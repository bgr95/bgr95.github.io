Toolkit for Focus [for ADHD]


Stuff related to focus, and focus issues, especially useful for people with ADHD. Mainly structured around Huberman Lab's podcast.



When we try to focus, certain neural paths responsible for the task should become more active. Now the 'activation' involves 3 main neurotransmitters and neuromodulators.

1. Norepinephrine/noradrenaline [as Neurotransmitter]
Released by locus coeruleus (which contains large number of noradrenaline producing neurons), it increases alertness of brain. The neurons are communicating, the brain gets ready to recieve and process information.
This alertness needs a direction.

2. Acetylcholine [as Neuromodular]
Acetylcholine binding to and activates certain receptors. It directs the alertness to particular neural pathways needed for the task. Acetylcholine also increases memory & learning.

3. Dopamine [as Neuromodulator]
Dopamine modulates various pathways including, cognition, executive functions, reward-motivation, etc. The part important for focus is the reward-motivational system. It provides the motivation to stay focussed. Dopamine can also act as a neurotransmitter, taking noradrenaline's place.

People with ADHD have issues with their Dopamine systems.



Body works in cycles, the ultradian rhythm, the circadian rhythm, and the infradian rhythm. These include cycles of hormone production, such as dopamine. So the first step is to optimize the cycles. Sleeping at correct time, waking up at correct time, etc.

1. Circadian Rhythm Optimization [24Hr Cycle]
Our bodies are optimized to function better in the morning, so it is ideal to wake up in the morning.

Sleep 8Hr+: This makes sure all the hormonal productions are optimized, and body is well rested.
Light Exposure: Light exposure, preferably early in the morning increases the releases of cortisol, it increases glucose in blood for body to utilize. Cortisol also learning increases retention.
Cold Shower: This increases noradrenaline levels by upto 3x.

2. Ultradian Rhythm Optimization [90-120Min Cycle]
The ideal duration of focus is 90-120Mins.

Sounds: Binaural beats at 40Hz, or white noise, amplifies the activity of prefrontal cortex, mainly trying to find patterns. It directs focus.
Deliberate Defocus [Yoganidra/Hypnosis/Short Naps]: It's useful to replenish the neurotransmitters and modulators before a session.

Other Behavioral Optimizations
The ideal duration of focus is 90-120Mins.

Deliberate Stress [Argue/Debate/Fasting/Cold Shower]: Argue with someone about some nonsense lol, gets you motivated.
Practicing Refocus [Meditation/Trataka]: This increases the ability to refocus. Same circuits are involved for leanring, as those for visual focus, so practicing to focus visually on things at right distance (same distance as reading a book) can be useful.
Weight Lifting: Near amphetamine levels of improvements reported by practitioners.



L Tyrosine [500mg]: It's a precursor to dopamine.
Alpha GPC [300-600mg]: Increases acetylcholine acutely.
Bacopa Moneiri/Brahmi [500mg]: Increases Choline.
Caffeine [<200mg]: Increases noradrenaline levels in the brain, blocks adenosine, and increases the number of dopamine receptors.
Omega 3 [1-3gm EPA]: Optimizes the production of stuff.
Creatine [3-5gm EPA]: Optimizes the production of stuff, via creatine phosphate system.
B50: Vitamin Bs are very useful.
Nicotine [4mg]: Works similar to acetylcholine and also increases dopamine.



The ADHD medication landscape is a whole another beast on its own. The ADHD medications try to fix the dopaminergic system. In such cases, the ultradian rhythms can last upto 4 hours.
