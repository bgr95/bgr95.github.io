Creativity


Creativity is the ability to form recognize new patterns in the observations. Mainly structured around Huberman Lab's podcast.



Creativity involves 3 main neuronal networks within the brain.

1. Executive Network [Controlling the Flow]
Executive function is the ability to govern our thinking and behavior in deliberate way. Mainly located in the prefrontal cortex. It helps suppress choices.

2. Default Mode Network [Processing Available Info]
Important for spontaneuous imagination. Connecting dots with the information already available to the brain.

3. Salience Network [Weightage]
Acts as a weightage system which assigns to select the idea to pursue.



Body works in cycles, the ultradian rhythm, the circadian rhythm, and the infradian rhythm. These include cycles of hormone production, such as dopamine. So the first step is to optimize the cycles. Sleeping at correct time, waking up at correct time, etc.

1. Circadian Rhythm Optimization [24Hr Cycle]
Our bodies are optimized to function better in the morning, so it is ideal to wake up in the morning.

Sleep 8Hr+: This makes sure all the hormonal productions are optimized, and body is well rested.
Light Exposure: Light exposure, preferably early in the morning increases the releases of cortisol, it increases glucose in blood for body to utilize. Cortisol also learning increases retention.
Cold Shower: This increases noradrenaline levels by upto 3x.

2. Ultradian Rhythm Optimization [90-120Min Cycle]
The ideal duration of focus is 90-120Mins.

Sounds: Binaural beats at 40Hz, or white noise, amplifies the activity in the prefrontal cortex, mainly trying to find patterns. It directs focus.
Deliberate Defocus [Yoganidra/Hypnosis/Short Naps]: It's useful to replenish the neurotransmitters and modulators before a session.

3. Other Behavioral Optimizations
The ideal duration of focus is 90-120Mins.

Deliberate Stress [Argue/Debate/Fasting/Cold Shower]: Easiest way to increase stress is to argue with someone about some nonsense (lol), gets you motivated.
[Maybe this is an ADHD adaptive behavioral self-medication?]
Practicing Refocus [Meditation/Trataka]: This increases the ability to refocus. Same circuits are involved for learning, as those for visual focus, so practicing to focus visually on things at right distance (same distance as reading a book) can be useful.
Weight Lifting: Increbibly powerful.



L Tyrosine [500mg]: It's a precursor to dopamine.
Alpha GPC [300-600mg]: Increases acetylcholine, for instant release type situation.
Bacopa Moneiri/Brahmi [500mg]: Reduces breakdown of acetylcholine.
Caffeine [<200mg]: Increases noradrenaline levels in the brain, blocks adenosine (keeps awake), and increases the number of dopamine receptors.
Omega 3 [1-3gm EPA]: Improves overall brain health.
Creatine [3-5gm EPA]: Good, but I don't know how.
Vitamin B [B50]: Act as metabolic support, makes the neurons function as they should.
Nicotine [4mg]: Works similar to acetylcholine and also increases dopamine. But personally makes me feel like dogshit.
