Toolkit for Fatloss


After a certain % of bodyfat, the body tries to maintain an equilibrium between stored energy, and metabolically active tissue utilizes the stored energy, such as muscles, etc. So, just keeping track of calories is sufficient to reach 10-15% body fat. After that we have to optimize stuff, to tilt the equilibrium in our favour, and reduce protein catabolism in the muscles. This is where we need a better understanding of how fatloss works is needed.



Fat accumulation and loss are mainly controlled by two hormones, insulin and glucagon, both released by pancreas. The other important hormones involved in fatloss are adrenaline and cortisol.

1. Insulin [Fat Accumulation]
Insulin makes the body store resources. In presence of nutrient in the blood stream, insulin is released, which attaches to insulin receptors in cells, allowing the cells to absorb nutrients.

2. Glucagon [Fat Metabolization]
Glucagon makes body metabolize fats, inorder to make it available to be used as energy. So, when fatty acids are present in the blood stream, glucagon makes it useable.

3. Adrenaline [Fat Mobilization]
Adrenaline attaches to and activates alpha and beta 'adrenergic' receptors in cells. The beta receptors make the cells release fats to the bloodstream and alpha receptors block the release. They are in an equilibrium, and for fatloss we should try to move this equilibrium position.

4. Cortisol [Glucose]
Cortisol is a stress hormone which is released, to prepare the body for emergencies. That means that it acts as a catalyst for catabolic processes that turns proteins to glucose via gluconeogenesis. Cortisol keeps blood glocose levels high, and this can make fatcels absorb extra glucose.

Other hormones such as Ghrelin, Leptin and many others are also involved in fatloss, accumulation. But we will not divert our attention too much.
