Arguably much of the evolutionary success of humans can be attributed to our ability to domesticate. Domestication helped us overcome the constraint of food, security and much more. But it is not all good when it comes to domestication. In this essay I want to look at some of the possible downsides of domestication.

Mechanistically, domestication is due to the ability of humans to manipulate levels of neurotransmitters in different species, that allow us to make them do things they wouldn't otherwise. In this essay we will not discuss this side of the issue.

Pros of Domestication

Before dissing on domestication, we have to discuss some of the advantages it gives us. Although domestication can be discussed in very generality, we will look at it via an example, Dogs.

Domestication of dogs has had a very deep impact on humans. Domestication has allowed dogs to be trained to perform various tasks and functions, such as assisting people with disabilities, guarding homes and livestock, and providing companionship. This has made them valuable and useful members of human society.

Domestication has allowed us to outsource some of the tasks required for survival.

Domestication has also been advantageous for the dogs.
Domestic dogs are able to live in close proximity to humans, which provides them with a safe and stable environment. This can also give them access to regular meals and medical care, which can improve their overall health and lifespan.

This has made dogs incredibly successful evolutionarily. A simple proof is that there are far more dogs in the world than there were ever wolves.

The take away is that domestication allows us to direct evolution of species that increases our own evolutionary success.

Cons of Domestication

While domestication has its advantages, the same advantages can act as a disadvantage in many ways. The human directed evolution poses different constraints of evolution compared to that posed by nature.

The safety and stability domestication offers the domesticated species shields it from evolutionary constraints. This allows mutations that would have been otherwise selected out by evolution to stay in the populations. As a result the domesticated species are far more likely to have genetic anamolies. As a result a domesticated animal will be far less likely to survive on its own in a chaotic nature.

Another disadvantage is that the survival of a dog is entirely dependent on its masters desires which the dog cannot control. While the dog has outsourced its safety and stability to humans, it has lost some of its own free will to humans.

Maybe this can be thought of as trading free will for safety and stability. The dog don't get to make decisions on their own free will or have consequences of their decisions.

We are now coowners of the souls of domesticated animals. So, in this sense I feel, domestication is a voluntary or involuntary loss of free will.

Evolutionary success over individuality? Is it a good trade?
